About Me

Hi, I'm Taiz. I'm a small-time programmer and Computer Science student. When I'm not dealing with my work or school obligations, I make some pretty cool things!

This site here is entirely static html with a teeny bit of css/js for some functionality. Though, if you were to disable both of those things, the site would display just fine. One of the most disheartening things to me is how big the web has gotten in the past decade, so I made a site akin to what I believe the web should be.

How long have you been programming for?

About 5 years now, minus a year or two in the time I've taken off from it to pursue other interests. My first programming language was Javascript (pre-ES6) to create some small mods for a game with my friends. After that I learned HTML (hence the 'Web' in my name) and built some basic sites for people. I then picked up python, built some cool things, and taught myself C and gained a solid understanding of the things we're spoiled with thanks to modern languages. My current endeavors mostly focus on Lua, what I consider to be my favorite programming language, as I develop a game.

Where does "Taiz" come from?

I'm not even sure on that one. I came up with it because I wanted a short pseudonym to go by online. I think everyone should have a right to anonymity (especially with all the doxing that's become commonplace). Taiz worked, felt nice, and was something I wouldn't mind being called forever. It's also a city in Yemen, apparently.

What's with your fixation on Toon Link drinking coffee?

A friend on my (now dead) Twitter sent me the image and said it reminded him of me. I thought it'd be funny to go a week with it as my profile picture, and I guess I ended up getting attached to it.

What do you use to code?

Initially, I lot of things. I started with a basic android text editor back when I was first starting out, then moved to Sublime Text (which everyone and their brother was recommending to me). Then everyone and their brother started recommending me VSCode (which was nice since it had a sweet terminal built right in). Then finally I moved to Vim, my current editor for the past three years, and can't imagine myself using anything else.

What operating system do you run?

Linux (hold the interjection) near exclusively. In specific, Xubuntu. I've been told Fedora or even Debian would be better for me, but honestly, I'm too lazy to reinstall my OS and would rather use something tried and true. As for my desktop environment, I use XFCE because I like how customizable/lightweight it is.
